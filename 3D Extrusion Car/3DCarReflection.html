3D Car Reflection

This is a reflection of the 3D car project, in which we modeled and developed 3D cars. After completing a process that included taking measurements, sketching, and modeling on design software, we used 3D printers to develop our toy car replicas. .

Project Guidelines

These were the guidelines for the project:

  • Car designed as an intersection of three forms
  • Ratio of designed car is 1:1
  • Size accuracy ±1%
  • Design exported as STL
  • Cura is used to orient model for 3D printing and minimize support material
  • File exported as a GCODE
  • Final design shared as an embedded STL on an HTML webpage

Project Steps

These were the project steps we took to complete the process, from taking measurements, to printing the final car:

  1. Measuring and drawing toy car model to scale
  2. Using the engineering drawing to make a 3D model of the car
  3. Printing the model
  4. Documenting the process through a CAD drawing and finding margin of error

The Process

Here was my process for creating the 3D car as I went through the project steps:

I began by choosing a toy to model my 3D creation off of and proceeded to use a caliper to take measurements of the length, width, and height in order to create a model that was to scale. With these measurements, I created an engineering drawing, showing the car from the front, side, and top angles, as well as from a 3D view.

With the design software OnShape, I used my engineering drawing as a reference to make a 3D model of the car. Through this process, I drew profiles on each side plane and used the extrusion tool to remove negative spaces from the model as well as the fillet tool to smooth out the edges. Once the foundation of the car was finished, I expanded my knowledge of the software to add wheel wells, a front windshield, and two windows to both sides of the car.
